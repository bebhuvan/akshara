What Makes This Different

Libre Baskerville

A warm, characterful serif with optical sizing. Not clinical like IBM Plex, not generic like Crimson. Perfect for extended reading with real personality.

Warm Paper Palette

Cream (#fffef9), not stark white. Terracotta accent, not blue. Smoke and ash grays with warmth. Feels like reading a beautiful old book.

Reading Time

Every chapter shows estimated reading time. Progress tracking shows chapters completed and percentage through the book. You know where you are.

Focus Mode

Press F to hide everything except text. True distraction-free reading. Press F again to exit. Also works with Esc.

Keyboard Shortcuts

T for TOC, S for settings, F for focus, arrows for chapters. Visual hints appear on first use. Power users will love this.

Position Memory

Leave mid-chapter, come back later—you're right where you left off. LocalStorage tracks your position automatically.

Italic Emphasis

Not just bold headings everywhere. Italics in the hero, in quotes, in book titles. More sophisticated, more editorial.

Numbered Collections

Huge ghosted numbers (01, 02, 03...) add visual rhythm to the collections grid. Subtle but distinctive.

Sticky Cover

Book cover stays in view as you scroll the hub page. Small detail, big impact on the feeling of browsing.

Design Principles

Warmth Over Coldness

Paper tones, not stark white. Terracotta, not clinical blue. This is literature, not a database—the design should feel alive.

Character Through Typography

Libre Baskerville brings personality. Italics add emphasis. Dramatic scale creates hierarchy. Type does the heavy lifting.

Thoughtful Interactions

Hover states that lift elements up. Links that underline on hover. Keyboard shortcuts for power users. Animations with purpose.

Reading First, Always

Focus mode removes everything. Progress tracking helps navigation. Reading time sets expectations. Position memory remembers where you were. Typography optimized for hours of reading.

Subtle Details

Custom selection color. Print styles. Smooth scroll behavior. Sticky elements. Shadow on hover. Each detail considered.

Not Minimal, Essential

We don't remove things for aesthetics—we remove distractions from reading. What remains serves a purpose.
