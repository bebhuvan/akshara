Mahatma Gandhi

1869 – 1948

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule. His philosophy of satyagraha and commitment to truth and non-violence profoundly influenced civil rights and freedom movements worldwide.

Life & Work

1893

Arrival in South Africa

Experienced racial discrimination that awakened his consciousness about injustice and led to his development of satyagraha philosophy.

1909

Hind Swaraj Published

Wrote his political manifesto during a voyage from London to South Africa, outlining his vision for Indian self-rule.

1915

Return to India

Established Sabarmati Ashram in Ahmedabad and began organizing peasants, farmers, and laborers against oppressive taxation.

1927

Autobiography Published

My Experiments with Truth published in English translation, bringing his philosophy to a global audience.

1930

Salt March

Led the 240-mile Salt March to protest British salt monopoly, galvanizing India's independence movement.

1942

Quit India Movement

Launched the movement demanding immediate British withdrawal from India during World War II.
